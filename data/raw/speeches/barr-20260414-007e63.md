# Rural Communities: Worth the Investment — barr — 2026-04-14

Governor Michael S. Barr, at Strengthening America's Economy through Rural Investment: A Working Forum, Washington, D.C., April 14, 2026.

Governor Barr opened by reflecting on his career work with rural communities, particularly regarding capital access. He noted that the level of innovation and creativity behind many of those solutions deserves greater recognition and sharing across regions.

Barr detailed his travels to rural areas, including the Mississippi Delta where he observed a struggling bank donate its building to a community development financial institution. He also visited the Travelers Hotel in Clarksdale, redeveloped through tax credits, and discussed the Delta Philanthropy Forum's role in infrastructure investment. On tribal lands, he encountered challenges around affordable mortgages on trust lands and heard about labor availability and production cost uncertainties affecting farmers and institutional anchors like hospitals.

## Rural Communities Are Diverse but Face Common Challenges

Barr emphasized that rural America is not one story but many, with economies tied to local industries ranging from agriculture to manufacturing. Physical distances and proximity to urban centers vary significantly, as do economic outlooks. Some communities struggle with workforce shortages while others adapt through economic diversification.

## Intrinsic Constraints in Rural Areas

Research shows population decline and aging burden rural regions. Between 2010 and 2023, residents 65+ grew from 7.4 million to 9.7 million. Migration became critical: over 86 percent relied on net migration for growth between 2020-2025. Communities face loss of essential institutions—banks, hospitals, colleges—and employment declines in agriculture and manufacturing. Over one-third of rural renters experience cost burdens with housing.

## Success Stories in Rural Economic Development

DeWitt, Arkansas transformed after a shoe plant closure and failed biofuel project. The community shifted focus from attracting one large employer to building locally-owned businesses, emphasizing tourism and outdoor retail. This revitalization was not driven by a single project but by a combination of mindset change, local ownership, economic diversification. Thomas and Davis, West Virginia reinvented themselves around arts and outdoor recreation. A CDFI provided loans, grants, and technical assistance while aggregating federal funding from multiple sources, creating a networked development model. CDFIs also improved mortgage access in Indian Country through character-based underwriting, achieving a 400 percent increase in loans on reservations.

## Recent External Developments Affecting Rural Communities

Trade policy and geopolitical events burden agricultural-dependent regions. International partners purchased approximately $175 billion in agricultural products in 2024—roughly 20 percent of U.S. production. Soybeans face particular concentration, with over 40 percent exported. Recent tariff disruptions reduced U.S. soybean exports to China by 38 percent in early 2025 versus 2024. Steel and aluminum tariffs increased machinery costs. Middle Eastern conflicts disrupted fertilizer supplies. Urea prices rose about 55 percent since the beginning of the year. Diesel prices climbed 50 percent annually, pressuring cattle and dairy farmers through both direct and indirect costs.

## Longer-Term Trends Affecting Rural Communities

Emerging trends include data center expansion, shifts toward high-skilled services, and artificial intelligence adoption. Research suggests over half of agricultural employment could experience substantial productivity gains through the adoption of AI-enabled technologies. However, rapid data center expansion in rural areas brings trade-offs: high energy and water demands, infrastructure strain, and uncertain job quality and quantity. Broader structural economic shifts away from goods production toward knowledge-intensive sectors disproportionately affect rural regions lacking capital, connectivity, and skilled workforces, widening rural-urban gaps.

## The Federal Reserve and Rural Communities

The Federal Reserve supports healthy economies serving all Americans through monetary policy promoting maximum employment and stable prices. Understanding rural labor markets and agricultural economies contributes to inclusive growth. The Federal Reserve System maintains nationwide presence ensuring diverse perspectives inform decisions. Community development teams at each Federal Reserve Bank and the Board of Governors engage communities through local sessions, regional convenings, and national conferences like the National Community Investment Conference in Phoenix. The Federal Reserve conducts original research through the Survey of Household Economics and Decisionmaking, Survey of Community Perspectives, and Small Business Credit Survey, complementing quantitative data with qualitative research. Community Reinvestment Act implementation encourages institutions to meet community needs, with rural revitalization activities playing important roles. Barr concluded that when multiple sources of capital come together, when public, private, and philanthropic partners align, communities can truly thrive.
