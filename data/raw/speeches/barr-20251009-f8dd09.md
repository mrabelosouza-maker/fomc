# The Economic Outlook and Monetary Policy — barr — 2025-10-09

Governor Michael S. Barr, at the Economic Club of Minnesota Luncheon, Golden Valley, Minnesota, October 9, 2025.

The FOMC reduced policy rates by 25 basis points in September. I supported the Committee's decision on September 17 to lower the federal funds rate by 25 basis points.

## Inflation Assessment

12-month headline inflation based on personal consumption expenditures (PCE) rose again in August to 2.7 percent. The tariff hikes have boosted core goods inflation, and at the same time progress on core services inflation has stalled. Research by Federal Reserve staff and others indicates that the increase in inflation since April has likely owed largely to the sharp increase in tariffs that kicked in around then. Effective tariff rates reached approximately 11 percent by August.

Barr expressed concern that temporary tariff effects could become persistent if businesses and consumers begin expecting higher inflation. The median FOMC participant estimates that headline PCE inflation will not return to our 2 percent target until the end of 2027, more than two years from today.

## Labor Market Conditions

Despite an unemployment rate of 4.3 percent, there are concerning signs including deteriorating household perceptions of job-finding prospects and rising unemployment among Black workers and younger demographics. The payroll services firm ADP found that private-sector employment shrank last month, in keeping with a slowdown in job creation since May. Other measures suggest that labor supply and demand remain in the same rough balance they have been in for more than a year.

## Monetary Policy Stance

The Brainard principle holds that when there is considerable uncertainty about the consequences of a policy action, the recommended course is to move more gradually. I believe that principle applies now, and that the FOMC should be cautious about adjusting policy so that we can gather further data.
